Text / Font

Common Text / Fonts and tips on using them

Make sure to use good Line Spacing, Tracking, and Kerning with your text!

  • Line Spacing: Line spacing, also known as Leading, refers to the distance between two lines of text.
  • Tracking: Tracking is the space between the letters and the words.
  • Kerning: Kerning is the space between two letters in the text. You typically will not need to worry about kerning because a well-designed typeface already has optimal kerning built in. It is most important when you want to adjust the kerning in a title or header to achieve the design you want.

Text Styles
Typical default text size for headings (1em = 16px)

  • <h1>

      2em / 32px  typically used for main page heading

  • <h2>

      1.5em / 24px  typically used for main page subheadings

  • <h3>

      1.17em / 18.72px  typically used for subheadings of subheadings

  • <h4>

      1em / 16px  emphasizing titles to articles, sections, or images/video
  • <h5>
      .83em / 13.28px  emphasizing titles to articles, sections, or images/video
  • <h6>
      .67em / 10.72px  emphasizing titles to articles, sections, or images/video
Note: <p> default font-size is 1em = 16px

Some basic Font-families

  • Serif fonts have a small stroke at the edges of each letter. They create a sense of formality and elegance.
  • Sans-serif fonts have clean lines (no small strokes attached). They create a modern and minimalistic look.
  • Monospace fonts - here all the letters have the same fixed width. They create a mechanical look.
  • Cursive fonts imitate human handwriting.
  • Fantasy fonts are decorative/playful fonts.
